Son of Grid Engine

The Son of Grid Engine (SGE) is a popular scheduler for High Performance Computing (HPC) clusters and other shared computing resources. In this lesson you will learn why clusters use SGE and how to use it to get the most out of your HPC systems.

Prerequisites

This lesson assumes you know your way around a bash prompt and that you have an account you can access on a cluster running SGE.

Getting ready

Once you have logged into your cluster you should be good to go!

Topics

  1. Cluster Architecture
  2. Why Use a Scheduler
  3. Running interactive jobs
  4. Submit jobs to the Queue from the Command Line
  5. Submit scripts as jobs to the queue.
  6. Submit parallel jobs
